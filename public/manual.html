Platina32 Song Contest

Everything you need to run a contest from start to finish.

01

Before the Contest

Before opening the app, make sure you have collected the following information from all participants:

→
Participant name
The forum username or display name of each contestant.
→
Represented country
Each contestant represents one country. No two contestants can share the same country.
→
Artist & song title
The song entry submitted by each contestant.
Tip: Publish all songs to participants before the contest starts so everyone has had a chance to listen before voting begins.
02

Setting Up the Contest

Go to platina32.com and click the Song Contest tile to open the app.

1
Enter a contest title
This appears in the scoreboard header and on all exported files. Example: MU Worldvision Song Contest 47
2
Choose a scoring system
Select Eurovision (12-10-8-7-6-5-4-3-2-1), Formula 1 (25-18-15-12-10-8-6-4-2-1), or define your own custom point values.
3
Add all contestants
Select each contestant's country from the dropdown, then fill in their name, artist, and song. Click Add Contestant after each one. Already-chosen countries are greyed out automatically.
4
Reorder if needed
Drag the ⠿ handle on each row to reorder contestants. This controls the voting order. Click Sort A–Z to sort by country name. The default order is the order contestants were added.
5
Click Start Contest
The button is enabled once you have enough contestants for the chosen scoring system (number of point positions + 1).
Note: You need at least one more contestant than the number of point positions in your scoring system. With Eurovision scoring (10 positions), you need at least 11 contestants.
03

Running the Voting Rounds

Each contestant takes a turn to cast their votes. The voting panel on the right shows whose turn it is.

1
Cast the votes of the current voter
The voter bar at the top shows who is currently voting. That contestant hands you his votes and you assign points to the contestants using the dropdowns.
2
All point slots must be filled
The Submit button stays disabled until every point value has been assigned to a different contestant. A voter cannot vote for themselves.
3
Click Submit Votes
Votes are locked in. The scoreboard updates immediately in the background, but the display stays unchanged until the reveal.
4
Run the reveal sequence
Click ▶ Show Reveal to see a preview of the current round's scoreboard changes. After submitting a round, click 🔗 Generate Snapshot Link to create a shareable link that contestants can open on their own devices to watch the reveal independently.
5
Repeat for each voter
The round counter in the header tracks progress. Repeat until all contestants have voted.
Tip: Made a mistake? Click ↩ Undo Last Round to revert the last submitted round and re-enter the votes.
Tip: At any point during the contest, you can click 📊 Export to export the current scoreboard state to an Excel file.
04

Ending the Contest

1
After the last round
Once the final voter has submitted and the reveal is done, the 🏆 End Contest & Show Results button appears.
2
Final results screen
A podium shows the top 3 contestants, followed by the full final standings table.
3
Save & share results
Click 💾 Save Results to store results locally for later. Click 🔗 Share Final Results to generate a shareable link. Click 📊 Export to Spreadsheet to download an Excel file.
Tip: Saved results can be viewed later from the setup page via the 📋 View Previously Finished Contest button — and reused to start a new contest with the same participants.
